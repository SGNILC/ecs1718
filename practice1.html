Araminta Ross

Moses

Born Araminta Ross, Harriet Tubman was a reknown for her service as a spy for the U.S during WWII, and was also when known as a conductor for the underground railroad. She was bore the nickname Moses, as she took part in the freedom of over 70 enslaved African Americans, transporting them from the South to the North. Although she passed away in 1913, her legacy and what she had done for the people whom she helped escape slavery, will never be forgotten, nor overlooked.

A few more notable accomplishments

  • Over a time period of 10 years, she helped free over 300 slaves.
  • After the Civil War, she married Nielson Davis (he was a Union Sholdier).
  • Held fund-raisers and worked at shelters for Churches who served as shelters for people negatively affected by the war.
  • She was a scout for Montgomery's Combahee River Raid a mission which freed about 500 slaves.
  • Found properties for the old to live in when they had nowhere else to stay.
