My Education

Jordan Communit School

The first school that I attended in the U.S was Jordan Community School. Located at 7414 N Wolcott Ave, Chicago, IL, Joran was where my first friendships began. The high light of this school was the afterschool program which I was in called Project J.A.M. The J.A.M stood for "Justice Actually Matters". That program is where I met some of the most amazing people I know. The adults and youth that set examples for kids like me are people who've influenced me to become the man I am today.

George B. Armstrong

Armstrong was the school that I transferred to in 7th grade. The best thing about this school was its people. The friends I made here, they were great people. Not only did they bring me lots of laughter, but they were also there for me when I needed them, and with the amount of work we got, most of us needed each other to pass tests. Although I don't speak to as much people anymore, I wouldn't mind going back to the simple days.

Lane Tech College Prep

Lane is the biggest school I've been to. There are over 4,000 people in the building everyday. A lot of my classmates from Armstrong go here, but the school is physically so big that we're all dispered and rarely see each other. Not to mention, my schedule doesn't correlate with most of my friends'. Other than that, I like this school so far. However, there have been a couple things that've occured that's made me want to fight someone, but I've calmed down.
