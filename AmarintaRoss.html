Araminta Ross

Moses

Born Amrinta Ross, Harriet Tubman was a reknown for her service as a spy for the U.S during WWII, and was also when known as a conductor for the underground railroad.

She was bared the nickname, Moses, as she took part in the freedom of over 70 enslaved African Americans, transporting them from the South to the North.

Although she passed away in 1913, her legacy and what she had done for the people whom she helped escape slavery, will never be forgotten, nor overlooked.
